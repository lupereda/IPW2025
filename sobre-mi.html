ABOUT ME

MY JOURNEY & PHILOSOPHY

Who I Am

I'm Lucía Pereda, a passionate project manager with a knack for organizing and leading teams to success. With a background in business administration and over 8 years of experience in the tech industry, I specialize in agile methodologies and efficient workflow management.

My journey in project management began in 2016 when I joined a fast-growing startup as a junior project coordinator. What started as curiosity quickly became a passion as I discovered my ability to connect technical teams with business objectives.

Over the years, I've had the privilege of working on diverse projects ranging from e-commerce platforms to cloud migrations and mobile applications. Each project has taught me valuable lessons about adaptability, communication, and the importance of understanding both the big picture and the smallest details.

My Philosophy

"My goal is to bridge the gap between technical teams and business objectives, ensuring that projects are delivered on time, within scope, and exceed client expectations."

I believe that successful project management isn't just about following methodologies and processes—it's about understanding people, fostering collaboration, and creating an environment where teams can do their best work.

I approach every project with three core principles:

  • Transparency: Open communication builds trust and helps identify issues before they become problems.
  • Adaptability: No two projects are the same, and flexibility is key to navigating challenges.
  • Empowerment: Great results come from empowered teams who feel valued and heard.

Beyond Work

When I'm not managing projects, you can find me exploring new technologies, hiking in the mountains of Patagonia, or indulging in my love for photography. I'm particularly passionate about landscape and street photography, which has taught me patience and the importance of timing—skills that translate well into project management!

I'm also an avid reader, especially of books on leadership, productivity, and behavioral psychology. Some of my favorites include "Atomic Habits" by James Clear and "The Five Dysfunctions of a Team" by Patrick Lencioni.

As a proud Argentine, I love sharing mate with friends and hosting asados on weekends. There's something special about bringing people together over good food and conversation—much like building strong project teams!
